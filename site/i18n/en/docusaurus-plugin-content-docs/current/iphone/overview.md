# Start on iPhone

The CodexPocket iPhone app is the control surface for the Codex environment running on your Mac. From the iPhone side, you add a Host, choose a Project, resume an existing Thread or start a new one, and send follow-up instructions from Composer.

## What to understand first on iPhone

- `Host`
  the Mac you are connecting to
- `Project`
  the workspace-level anchor for threads, branches, skills, and commands
- `Thread`
  the main surface for transcripts and follow-up turns
- `Composer`
  the entry point for text turns, slash commands, skills, and exec

## What this section covers

- what the iPhone side should confirm before the first pairing
- how to look at Projects and Threads after QR import
- how to think about branches, exec, and follow-up from iPhone
