Once the Mac side is ready, the next step is the iPhone. In the iPhone app, you add the Mac as a `Host`, then open `Projects` and `Threads`.

## Main screens you use on iPhone

- `Home`
  The entry point for the default Host, recent `Projects`, and the thread you were using last.
- `Sidebar`
  The place to open `Projects`, `Hosts`, and `Settings`.
- `Projects`
  The list where you choose the workspace you want to open.
- `Hosts`
  The place to add a Mac, run `Connection Test`, and retry setup.
- `Settings`
  The place to adjust language, theme, font size, and connection-related behavior.

## What you do on the iPhone side

1. Launch the app.
2. Allow camera and local network access if needed.
3. Use `Add from QR` from the empty screen, or open `Hosts` from the sidebar and scan the Mac QR code.
4. Wait for the `Project` list to be imported automatically.
5. Open a `Project` and then a `Thread`.

The two ideas that matter most at the beginning are `Hosts` and `Projects`. If you understand those, the rest of the iPhone flow becomes easy quickly.
